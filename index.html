432 Victims, No Perpetrators? Statistics from AIC Explained.

7 Jun 2020, 7:54PM | 218 words | 1 minute
Last Saturday, Australia saw tens-of-thousands of protestors take to the streets demanding justice for Indiginous Australians. This was inspired by the ongoing protests against police brutality and treatment of African-Americans. Shortly after the beginning of the protests, similar evidence of mistreatment of Indiginous Australias went viral resulting in a surge of outage. In particular, this evidence was an assertion that over 400 Indiginous Australians have died in Police Custody since 1991, a statistic that has now been shared hundreds of thousands of times, widely accepted as true. On first glance, devoid of context this statistic seems pretty bad, a national tradegy. What many people don't (or perhaps, don't want to realise) is that number comes with a number of caveats that significantly discredits the fundemental reason for the outrage of this number. In this post I will investigate this data in more detail, contrasting the results of research into deaths in custody in Australia with the narrative that has been used to underpin mass-demonstrations across the country.
